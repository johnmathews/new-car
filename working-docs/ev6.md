## ⚠️ Important: Pre-facelift vs Facelift (2025 MY)

The EV6 received a significant mid-cycle facelift, revealed **14 May 2024** and arriving in Europe from late 2024. The two versions differ meaningfully — see `model-year-guide.md` for full details.

| | Pre-facelift (MY 2021–2024) | Facelift (MY 2025+) |
|---|---|---|
| **Battery** | 77.4 kWh gross / 74 kWh usable | **84 kWh gross** |
| **Range (WLTP RWD)** | ~504 km | ~583 km |
| **Infotainment** | Older Kia system | New ccNC — faster processor, OTA updates |
| **Interior** | Original curved dual screen | Updated curved screen, D-shaped wheel, fingerprint auth |
| **Charging port location** | Right rear fender | **Left** rear fender |
| **Airbags** | 7 | **10** (B-pillar reinforced, rear side airbags added) |
| **Digital Key** | 1.0 | 2.0 (NFC card / smartphone) |
| **New ADAS** | — | Junction warnings, Evasive Steering Assist |
| **Star Map lighting** | ❌ | ✅ New front light design |
| **How to identify** | Registered 2024 or earlier (mostly) | Registered from late 2024; 84 kWh battery; left-side charge port |

**Used car implication:** Most 2022–2024 EV6s on Marktplaats are pre-facelift. Facelift models arrived in Europe late 2024, so some late-2024-registered cars may be facelift. When viewing, check the charge port location (right = old, left = new) and ask about battery size.

## Why it's #2

The EV6 has more features included as standard than any other option on the shortlist. If you find a GT-Line within budget, it's an excellent car. It loses to the Ioniq 5 mainly on price — equivalent spec consistently costs €3,000–5,000 more — and the cabin is very slightly noisier.

Shares the same 800V platform and 10-year battery warranty as the Ioniq 5 (they're built on the same underlying E-GMP architecture).

## Panoramic roof — a weakness vs the Ioniq 5 ⚠️

The EV6's roof option is more accurately described as a **moonroof** than a panoramic glass panel. This is the weakest of the three shortlisted cars on John's #2 priority (light and space). Key facts:

- Multiple sources describe it as a "moonroof", not a panoramic panel
- Noticeably smaller than the Ioniq 5's vision roof — one direct comparison: "EV6 has smaller windows, much darker interior colours and a smaller moonroof" vs the Ioniq 5
- Reduces front headroom by approximately 56mm (2.2 inches) — the Ioniq 5's fixed roof does not affect headroom at all
- The smaller glass, combined with the EV6's darker interior colour options, means less sense of light overall
- Available from GT-Line trim only

If panoramic glass and a sense of space is a top priority, the EV6 falls short compared to the Ioniq 5.

**Windscreen / upward visibility is also the weakest here.** Reviews specifically note the EV6's "aggressively angled windscreen pillars obscure your view at junctions and roundabouts." The more car-like, low-slung profile means a more raked windscreen and lower driving position than both the Enyaq and Ioniq 5. Seeing overhead traffic lights will be harder here than in either rival. None of the three have a panoramic windscreen like the Picasso's Zenith, but the EV6 is the furthest from that experience.

## Comfort & cabin

- **Noise level:** ~68–69 dB at motorway speed — slightly noisier than Ioniq 5 (road noise from the floor area)
- **Space:** Best rear legroom of the three — a genuine advantage for longer trips with kids
- **Seats:** Sporty shape, comfortable. Leather standard from GT-Line.

---

## Audio

Meridian 14-speaker system with subwoofer — best audio of the three cars. Notable step up over the Ioniq 5's Bose system if audio quality matters.

---

## Climate & pre-conditioning

- Dual-zone climate control
- Can pre-heat cabin via Kia Connect app
- **Same limitation as Ioniq 5:** Scheduled heating only works while actively charging
- Heat pump availability varies by trim — always verify

---

## Known reliability notes

The EV6 shares much of its engineering with the Ioniq 5, so similar things to watch:

1. **12V auxiliary battery** — same weak point as Ioniq 5. Check service history.
2. Check for any software recalls (Kia has issued several OTA updates)
3. Battery health report: expect >85% SoH under 100,000 km
4. Tyre wear — EVs are heavier and wear tyres ~20–30% faster

---

## Charging specifics

- **AC (home):** Up to 11 kW. Full charge overnight (~6–7 hours from 20%)
- **DC (fast):** Up to 233 kW. 10–80% in ~18 minutes
- Compatible with Type 2 (AC) and CCS (DC)
- Same charger compatibility as Ioniq 5 — all the same NL networks work

## Budget note

The EV6 GT-Line is the right trim but consistently sits at the top end of budget (€27,000–32,000). It's worth keeping an eye out for deals, but the Ioniq 5 Lounge likely offers similar features for €3–5k less on average.
