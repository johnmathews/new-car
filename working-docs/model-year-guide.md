# Model Year & Facelift Guide — Ioniq 5, EV6, Enyaq

_Last updated: April 2026 — based on web research_

This is the most important document to read before searching for used listings. All three cars have had significant mid-cycle facelifts that change features, software, and battery meaningfully. A 2023 and a 2025 version of the same model can be quite different cars.

---

## Quick reference: which year = which generation?

| Car | Pre-facelift | Facelift | Key divide |
|-----|-------------|----------|------------|
| **Skoda Enyaq** | 2021–2024 | 2025+ | Facelift revealed Jan 2025; NL deliveries from ~April 2025 |
| **Hyundai Ioniq 5** | 2021–2024 | 2025 MY+ | Launched Jul 2024; arrived in Europe late 2024/early 2025 |
| **Kia EV6** | 2021–2024 | 2025 MY+ | Revealed May 2024; arrived in Europe late 2024/early 2025 |

**Practical implication:** On Marktplaats today (spring 2026), most used cars for sale will be pre-facelift 2022–2024 models. Facelift models (2025 MY) will be newer, less common, and more expensive. They may exist but expect a significant price premium.

## Skoda Enyaq — Pre-facelift vs Facelift

### Pre-facelift (2021–2024)
- **Software:** Versions up to 4.0 — known to be sluggish and slow to respond. Infotainment hardware is older and **cannot be upgraded** to newer software versions (VW Group has changed the hardware twice now).
- **Electric tailgate:** Not available on ANY trim — not even as an option.
- **Blind spot monitoring:** Optional extra (Side Assist package required), not standard.
- **Tri-zone climate:** Standard.
- **Trims:** Base, Edition, Sportline, L&K.
- **Battery:** 82 kWh gross / 77 kWh usable.
- **Range (WLTP):** ~534 km.

### Facelift (2025+)
Revealed 8 January 2025. Orders open in Netherlands from 9 January 2025. NL deliveries from approximately April 2025. Starting price ~€42,990 new.

- **Software:** Version 6.0 with **new hardware**. Noticeably faster and more capable. Smart Dials (3 physical knobs with built-in 1.3" colour displays for temperature, volume, drive mode). Pre-facelift cars cannot receive this upgrade.
- **Electric tailgate:** ✅ **Now standard on ALL trims** — a complete reversal from pre-facelift.
- **Blind spot monitoring:** ✅ Now standard on all trims.
- **ACC:** Standard on all trims (was already standard before).
- **Tri-zone climate:** Standard on all trims.
- **New trim lineup:** SE L, Edition, Sportline. L&K has been dropped.
- **Battery:** Same 82 kWh gross / 77 kWh usable.
- **Aerodynamics improved:** Cd 0.264 → 0.245 (SUV); Cd 0.234 → 0.225 (Coupe).
- **Range (WLTP):** Up to ~589 km (improvement from aerodynamics, not battery size).
- **Remote park assist & trained parking:** New parking features.
- **New design:** Modern Solid face (inspired by Elroq), split headlights, flatter bonnet.

> ⚠️ **Used car implication:** A 2025-registered Enyaq in the Netherlands is very likely a facelift. Any 2024 or earlier is pre-facelift. Facelift models will be newer (registered from spring 2025) and more expensive on the used market.

## Hyundai Ioniq 5 — Pre-facelift vs Facelift

### Pre-facelift (2021–2024)
- **Software:** Older Hyundai infotainment (not ccNC). OTA updates supported, but not the new interface.
- **Battery:** 77.4 kWh gross / 72.6 kWh usable (standard), or 58 kWh on standard range.
- **Range (WLTP):** ~480 km (RWD LR), ~430 km (AWD).
- **Rear wiper:** Not present on most pre-facelift cars.
- **Wireless CarPlay:** Available on most trims already.
- **2024 model year:** Mostly a carry-over — only minor connectivity and safety updates. Not meaningfully different from 2023 for buying purposes.

### Facelift (2025 MY)
Launched globally July 23, 2024. Available in South Korea first, then the rest of the world through late 2024 and into 2025. Some 2024-registered European cars may be facelift spec.

- **Software:** New ccNC (Connected Car Navigation Cockpit) infotainment. Wireless OTA updates. Wireless Apple CarPlay & Android Auto as standard.
- **Battery:** ✅ **84 kWh gross** (up from 77.4 kWh) — ~8% more range.
- **Range (WLTP):** ~484–580 km depending on version (up ~30 km vs pre-facelift).
- **New interior:** Redesigned climate controls (physical buttons replacing some touch areas), new centre console, new steering wheel, larger dual touchscreen.
- **Rear wiper:** Now standard.
- **Rear seats:** Can now be folded remotely from the app.
- **Built-in dashcam:** Added.
- **Bumpers:** Restyled front and rear, new aerodynamic wheels.
- **Rear spoiler:** Extended by 50 mm.
- **New colour options:** Atlas White Matte, Ecotronic Gray Matte, Celadon Gray Matte.
- **2026 MY (Dec 2025):** Simplified trim structure — 4 trims instead of 16 variants. New Elite and Premium trims replace Dynamiq/Epiq. All now use 84 kWh battery.

> ⚠️ **Used car implication:** Pre-facelift Ioniq 5s (2022–2024) are widely available and priced well. A 2025-registered car is likely facelift spec with the bigger battery and new interior. For European used market, you may find late-2024-registered facelift examples, but most 2024-registered cars are pre-facelift.

## Kia EV6 — Pre-facelift vs Facelift

### Pre-facelift (2021–2024)
- **Software:** Older Kia infotainment. Received some OTA updates but not the new ccNC system.
- **Battery:** 77.4 kWh gross / 74 kWh usable.
- **Range (WLTP):** ~504 km (RWD), ~484 km (AWD).
- **Interior:** Dual 12.3" screens (curved dual screen), but older software.
- **Charging port:** Located on the right rear.

### Facelift (2025 MY)
Revealed 14 May 2024. Available in Europe late 2024/early 2025. UK received it first in Europe; Netherlands followed.

- **Software:** New ccNC (Connected Car Navigation Cockpit) — same next-gen system as Ioniq 5 facelift. Faster processor, better OTA support.
- **Battery:** ✅ **84 kWh gross** (up from 77.4 kWh).
- **Range (WLTP):** Up to ~583 km (RWD), better than pre-facelift.
- **New interior:** Updated curved panoramic screen layout, new D-shaped steering wheel, new dashboard material finish, fingerprint recognition system (unlock/start without key).
- **Digital Key 2.0:** Smartphone/NFC card to unlock and start the car.
- **New ADAS:** Junction Turning Vehicle warning, Junction Crossing Vehicle warning, Lane Change Side Vehicle, Evasive Steering Assist.
- **Safety upgrade:** 10 airbags (up from 7). B-pillar reinforced. Second-row side airbags added.
- **Charging port:** Moved to **left rear fender** (change from pre-facelift right-side location).
- **Star Map lighting:** New distinctive LED headlight design.
- **New design:** Sharper front bumper angles, revised rear.
- **GT model:** 641 hp (up from 576 hp).

> ⚠️ **Used car implication:** Pre-facelift EV6s (2022–2024) are widely available. Facelift (2025 MY) examples arrived in Europe late 2024, so some late-2024-registered cars may be facelift. Most 2024-registered cars are pre-facelift.

## Impact on rankings and which generation to buy

| | Pre-facelift | Facelift | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Enyaq** | Sluggish software, no tailgate, cheaper | Better software, tailgate standard, pricier | Facelift much better but expensive; pre-facelift OK if software isn't a dealbreaker |
| **Ioniq 5** | Smaller battery (77 kWh), older interior | Bigger battery (84 kWh), new interior, rear wiper | Facelift is a meaningful upgrade, but pre-facelift is still a solid, capable car |
| **EV6** | Smaller battery (77 kWh), older software | Bigger battery (84 kWh), new ccNC, more safety | Same as Ioniq 5 — facelift noticeably better but pre-facelift still works well |

### What this means for the shortlist

**Enyaq pre-facelift (2022–2024):** The sluggish software is a real-world annoyance confirmed by user reports. No electric tailgate matters less now (it's a nice-to-have), but the software gap is notable. These cars are cheaper and the best bet for staying within €20–27k budget.

**Enyaq facelift (2025+):** Solves the software and tailgate issues completely, but will be priced well above budget on the used market in spring 2026 (likely €35,000+).

**Ioniq 5 / EV6 pre-facelift:** The software situation is better than the Enyaq pre-facelift (Hyundai/Kia's older system wasn't particularly criticised). The main loss vs facelift is the smaller 77 kWh battery and the older interior. Still perfectly usable.

**Ioniq 5 / EV6 facelift (2025 MY):** 84 kWh battery, new interior, wireless CarPlay as standard. Will command a price premium but worth looking for if budget allows.

## Checklist additions: ask the seller

For any listing, add these to the pre-purchase checks:

- [ ] What model year / when was it first registered?
- [ ] Is this pre-facelift or facelift? (See table above for dates)
- [ ] For Enyaq: is the software version 6.0 or older? Is there an electric tailgate?
- [ ] For Ioniq 5 / EV6: is the battery 77 kWh or 84 kWh? Does it have the new ccNC screen?
