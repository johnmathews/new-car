# Tesla Model Y — Assessment

_Last updated: March 2026_
_Verdict: **Borderline viable — worth considering if the UX doesn't put you off**_

## What it does really well

**Boot space.** At 971 litres with seats up, the Model Y has by far the largest boot of any car on this list — larger than even the Enyaq (585 L). Combined with a completely flat floor and the frunk, it's genuinely a very practical family car.

**Charging network.** The Tesla Supercharger network is the most reliable and widely distributed fast-charging network in Europe. Stops are fast (up to 250 kW) and the navigation plans them automatically. For the UK and Belgium holidays, this is a real advantage.

**Range.** Consistently good real-world range. The Long Range version is one of the best-range EVs in its class.

**Value.** Used 2021–2023 examples start around €28,000 in the NL market — at the lower end of budget for a car with this much capability.

**Practical interior.** Flat floor, lots of storage, comfortable rear bench with good legroom.

## The significant concerns

### 1. Polarising UX — this is the main issue

Tesla's interior philosophy is radically different from every other car on this list:

- **No instrument cluster.** Speed, navigation, everything is on the central 15" touchscreen. You look right, not forward, to check your speed.
- **No physical stalks** (2024+ Juniper refresh). Indicators, wipers, and gear selection are all handled via the steering wheel or touchscreen. Many drivers find this distracting and counterintuitive.
- **No physical buttons** for climate, seat adjustment, or mirrors. Everything is buried in menus on the touchscreen.

This is a genuine point of division. Some people adapt quickly and love the simplicity. Others find it actively unsafe or just annoying after years of use. It's worth a test drive specifically to evaluate this — it can't be assessed from specs alone.

### 2. Build quality

Early Model Y (2020–2022) examples had documented panel gap, paint, and interior fit-and-finish issues. Later 2023+ cars and the 2024 Juniper refresh are significantly better, but it's worth inspecting carefully on any used example.

### 3. No traditional dealer network

Tesla sells and services direct. The closest Tesla service centre to Spaarndam is in Amsterdam. Over-the-air software updates handle many issues remotely, but physical service requires booking and potentially longer wait times than a traditional brand.

### 4. Resale volatility

Tesla has cut new car prices multiple times, which has pushed down used values unpredictably. Good if you're buying used now, less good for resale when you sell in 5 years.

## Features vs wishlist

| Feature | Status |
|---------|--------|
| Adaptive cruise control | ✅ Autopilot standard (very capable) |
| Electric tailgate | ✅ Standard |
| Panoramic glass roof | ✅ Standard (fixed glass, not opening) |
| Parking assist (automated) | ✅ Autopark standard |
| Reversing / 360° camera | ✅ Standard (multiple cameras) |
| Leather / premium seats | ⚠️ Vegan leather standard (not real leather) |
| Wireless phone charging | ✅ Standard |
| Wireless CarPlay | ❌ Tesla does not support CarPlay or Android Auto |
| Heated seats | ✅ Standard |
| Blind spot monitoring | ✅ Via camera-based system |

**Important:** Tesla does **not** support Apple CarPlay or Android Auto — ever. If your family uses CarPlay for navigation or music, you would need to use Tesla's own navigation system and Bluetooth audio instead. Worth factoring in.

## Charging specifics

- DC fast charging: up to 250 kW at V3 Superchargers
- Supercharger network: excellent in NL, Belgium, and UK
- Non-Tesla chargers: compatible via CCS adapter (included on newer cars)
- Home charging: up to 11 kW AC

## Verdict

The Model Y is a genuinely capable family car with the biggest boot, excellent charging, and competitive used pricing. It earns a place in the extended shortlist.

But it's not for everyone. The all-screen UX, absence of CarPlay, and Tesla's direct-service model are real friction points. A test drive is essential — particularly to evaluate the no-stalk, touchscreen-only interface on the 2024+ Juniper model.

If the UX works for your family, it's a strong contender. If it doesn't, it's easily ruled out.
