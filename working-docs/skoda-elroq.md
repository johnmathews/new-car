# Skoda Elroq — Assessment

_Last updated: March 2026_
_Verdict: **Not viable — over budget and smaller than the Enyaq it would replace**_

## What it is

The Elroq is Skoda's newest electric SUV, launched late 2024. It sits below the Enyaq in the range — roughly the electric equivalent of the Karoq. It's 20 cm shorter than the Enyaq, with most of that difference coming out of the boot.

## Key specs

| | |
|---|---|
| **New price (NL)** | From €34,990 |
| **Used price (NL)** | €33,685–52,000+ (barely any used stock) |
| **Battery options** | 52 kWh (Elroq 50), 63 kWh (Elroq 60), 82 kWh (Elroq 85) |
| **Range (WLTP, 85)** | ~560 km |
| **Charging (85)** | Up to 185 kWh DC |
| **Boot** | 470 L (vs 585 L on Enyaq) |
| **Wheelbase** | Same as Enyaq — rear legroom is comparable |

## Why it doesn't work

**Budget.** Used Elroqs start at around €33,685 — already above the €30,000 ceiling, and well above the comfortable range. The car is simply too new for used prices to have come down.

**Smaller boot.** At 470 L, it's smaller than the Enyaq (585 L) and notably smaller than the Ioniq 5/EV6 (~490 L). You'd be trading down on practicality compared to every other car on the shortlist — including the one you're replacing.

**Same structural problem as the Enyaq.** Like its bigger sibling, the Elroq almost certainly has no electric tailgate (Skoda's MEB-platform SUVs don't offer one). So you'd have a smaller, more expensive version of the Enyaq, without the Enyaq's main advantage (boot space).

**Barely any used stock.** The car only launched in late 2024. There are very few second-hand examples available in the Netherlands, so selection is poor and prices are barely below new.

## What it does well (for the record)

- Rear legroom is surprisingly good for its size (same wheelbase as Enyaq)
- Travel Assist 3.0 (2026 models) — good driver assist suite
- LFP battery option on lower variants (longer cycle life, can charge to 100% daily)
- Good value relative to the Enyaq when bought new

## Verdict

Ruled out. It's over budget, has a smaller boot, and doesn't resolve the main Enyaq concern (no electric tailgate). There's no scenario where you'd pick the Elroq over the Enyaq, let alone over the Ioniq 5 or EV6.

If the budget were higher and you wanted a smaller, more city-friendly EV, it might be worth a look — but that's not the brief here.
