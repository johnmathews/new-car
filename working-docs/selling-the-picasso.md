# Selling the Picasso

_Last updated: March 2026_
_Car: 2017 Citroën Grand C4 Picasso 1.2L PureTech, registration H416GS, ~174,000 km_

## Bottom line

**List on Marktplaats at €9,000–9,500. Expect to sell for €7,500–9,500 privately. Don't replace the timing belt.**

March–May is actually the best time of year to sell — do it now.

## The timing belt question — DON'T replace it

The 1.2L PureTech engine has a known design flaw: it uses a **wet timing belt** (runs in oil). These are known to stretch and fail prematurely, sometimes causing engine failure. It's a well-documented issue across the Citroën/Peugeot/Vauxhall group cars using this engine.

**The maths:**
- Timing belt replacement cost: ~€700
- Increase in resale value: ~€200–400 (buyers already know about this issue and price it in)
- Net result: you lose €300–500

**Advice:** Don't replace it. Disclose the mileage honestly. Buyers who know about the engine (many do) will factor it in regardless. Buyers who don't know will likely not pay more for a fresh belt.

If asked directly, be honest that it hasn't been replaced.

## What to include in the listing

**Photos to take:**
- Exterior: all four corners, front, rear, both sides
- Interior: dashboard, front seats, rear seats (show the space), boot
- Under the bonnet (shows you're transparent)
- Tyres (tread depth)
- Any minor damage (scratches, dents — show them to build trust)

**Info to include:**
- Full service history (if you have it)
- MOT (APK) expiry date
- Known issues (honest = faster sale, fewer time-wasters)
- Reason for selling (buying an EV — buyers appreciate this)
- Number of previous owners
- Mileage

## Marktplaats tips

- Price: start €9,000–9,500, set floor at ~€8,000
- Respond quickly to messages — serious buyers move fast
- Avoid "I'll pay your price but can you deliver to [far away]" — common scam
- Meet buyers in daylight, in a public place or at home
- Test drive: go with them, bring your driving licence
- Payment: cash or bank transfer before handing over keys. No PayPal, no Tikkie from strangers.
- Title transfer: go to RDW or a dealer together to transfer the registration (this protects you from liability)

---

## RDW registration transfer

When selling privately in NL, you must transfer the registration (tenaamstelling) together with the buyer. Options:
- At a dealership (they can handle it for a small fee)
- Via Mijn Overheid / DigiD (online, but both parties need DigiD)
- At an RDW-recognised point (garages, some supermarkets)

Don't hand over keys until the registration is formally transferred. You remain liable for the car (parking fines, accidents) until it's in the buyer's name.
