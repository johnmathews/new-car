# Nissan Ariya — Assessment

_Last updated: March 2026_
_Verdict: **Supplementary option — watch for deals, but likely over budget for the best spec**_

## Why it's here

The Ariya isn't a standout vs the Korean cars, but it's a genuine family SUV with a distinctive, premium interior and a growing used market. Worth including as a search filter on Marktplaats — if a well-specced example appears at the right price, it's worth a look.

The main constraint is price: used Ariyas with the larger battery (the one worth having) tend to sit at €28,000–38,000, which is at or above budget for the better trims.

## What it does well

**Interior quality.** The Ariya has one of the nicest cabins of any EV in this price range — clean, minimalist (in a good way), with flush door handles, a wide centre console that glides forward, and high-quality materials. Feels more premium than the Enyaq or ID.4 inside.

**Nissan ProPilot.** Good driver assist suite including ACC with lane centring. Works well on motorways.

**Wireless CarPlay.** Standard — works well.

**Comfortable ride.** Generally praised for ride quality and NVH — comparable to Ioniq 5.

## Concerns

**Charging speed.** At 130 kW DC (400V), it's the slowest DC charger on the shortlist — roughly 40 minutes for 10–80%. Fine for daily driving, but the UK/Belgium holiday stops will be longer than with the Korean 800V cars.

**Towing.** Only 750 kg — the lowest of any car on this list by a significant margin. Can't tow a bike trailer, small caravan, or any meaningful load. If towing is ever needed, this is a hard constraint.

**Boot.** At 468 L it's smaller than most alternatives at this price — notably smaller than the Enyaq (585 L), VW ID.4 (543 L), or Ioniq 5 (~490 L).

**Price for good spec.** The 87 kWh Evolve trim (the one worth having) tends to sit at €28,000–35,000 used. The 63 kWh version is cheaper but range and performance are noticeably worse — not recommended.

**Resale uncertainty.** Nissan's EV market position is less established than Hyundai/Kia. Resale values are harder to predict.

## Vs the main shortlist

It occupies similar ground to the Enyaq/ID.4 but is more expensive, slower to charge, and has a smaller boot and more limited towing. The interior is nicer, but that alone doesn't justify the premium over an Ioniq 5 Lounge.

**Worth buying if:** You find an 87 kWh Evolve under €27,000 — at that price it becomes genuinely competitive.
