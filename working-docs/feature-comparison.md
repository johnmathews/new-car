# Feature Comparison — Wishlist vs Cars

_Last updated: April 2026_

Feature priorities (from conversations, updated 8 April 2026):

1. **Predictive ACC (nav-based)** — adjusts speed for curves, roundabouts, speed limits, junctions. Ioniq 5: NSCC (standard all trims). EV6: NSCC-C (standard all trims). Enyaq: pACC (Assisted Drive package — if the Enyaq has ACC, it's predictive by design). [J: confirmed, much better than Picasso]
2. **Blind spot detection** — Ioniq 5: standard (all trims). EV6: standard (all trims). Enyaq: Side Assist package (option, often on Business Edition).
3. **Traffic sign recognition** — standard on ALL three cars, ALL trims. Camera + nav data. Shows speed limit in instrument cluster. Also feeds into predictive ACC to auto-adjust speed.
4. **Panoramic sunroof** — [J: BIG priority after test drive — see note below on types]. Ioniq 5: Lounge+ (option). EV6: GT-Line (standard). Enyaq: option (~€990).
5. **Reversing / 360° camera** — Ioniq 5: Lounge+. EV6: Wind+. Enyaq: all trims.
6. **Park assist (auto steering)** — car measures the space and steers into parallel/perpendicular spots, you handle pedals. Ioniq 5: Smart Parking Assist / SPA (Lounge+). EV6: SPA (GT-Line standard). Enyaq: Parking Plus package (Park Assist + 360° Area View Camera + Trained Parking) — NOT standard on any pre-2025 trim.
7. Electric tailgate — nice-to-have

> ### Panoramic windscreen (Picasso-style Zenith)?
>
> **None of the three have a panoramic windscreen.** The Picasso's Zenith windscreen — where the glass extends upward into the roof, letting you see overhead traffic lights and sky — is extremely rare in modern cars. No mainstream EV in this price bracket offers it. This is one area where no shortlisted car can replicate the Picasso experience.
>
> However, the **Enyaq** comes closest to the Picasso's upward visibility for a different reason: it has the **highest, most upright driving position** of the three. Multiple reviews confirm you sit noticeably higher in the Enyaq than in the Ioniq 5 or EV6, "looking down on anyone in an Ioniq 5 or EV6." The more upright seating + less raked windscreen = better ability to see overhead traffic lights and signs.
>
> The **EV6 is the worst** for upward visibility. Reviews specifically note its "aggressively angled windscreen pillars obscure your view at junctions and roundabouts." The more car-like profile means a more raked windscreen and lower seating position.
>
> The **Ioniq 5** sits between the two: higher than the EV6, lower than the Enyaq.
>
> ### Upward windscreen visibility ranking (seeing overhead traffic lights, signs, sky):
> 1. 🥇 **Enyaq** — highest seating position, most upright windscreen angle, most SUV-like profile
> 2. 🥈 **Ioniq 5** — moderate seating height, steeply raked but reasonable forward visibility
> 3. 🥉 **EV6** — lowest/most car-like, aggressively raked windscreen, visibility at junctions specifically criticised
>
> ### Panoramic roof glass ranked by size / light / sense of space (opening doesn't matter):
>
> **1. Enyaq Coupé** — full-length fixed glass roof, standard on all trims, described as the largest panoramic roof in Skoda's history. Runs from B-pillar to rear. No blind (glass is heat-treated/tinted). Very dramatic. ⚠️ Note: this is the Coupé body style, not the regular SUV John test drove.
>
> **2. Ioniq 5 (Lounge+)** — large fixed "vision roof", a single glass panel with no cross-bar supports. Reviewers consistently describe it as the best panoramic roof of this class for light and openness. "Incredibly expansive." Has motorised sunblind. Covers front and centre of the roof. The best option among the three main shortlisted cars (Enyaq SUV, Ioniq 5, EV6).
>
> **3. Enyaq Regular SUV (optional on Edition+)** — smaller than it sounds. Reviewer notes it "doesn't extend far over the rear seats" and a child in a rear-facing seat in the back may not see through it. Covers primarily the front of the cabin. Must be specified as an option — not standard.
>
> **4. EV6 (GT-Line)** — described by multiple sources as a "moonroof", not a true panoramic panel. Clearly smaller than the Ioniq 5's roof. One direct comparison review noted "the EV6 appears to have smaller windows, much darker interior colours and a smaller moonroof" vs the Ioniq 5. Also reduces front headroom by ~56mm.
>
> ### Combined visibility + light ranking (windscreen + roof together):
>
> | Car | Windscreen / upward view | Panoramic roof | Overall for light & space |
> |-----|-------------------------|----------------|--------------------------|
> | **Ioniq 5 Lounge** | 🥈 Good | 🥇 Best (large vision roof) | **🥇 Best overall for cabin light — including rear passengers (kids)** |
> | **Enyaq SUV (with option)** | 🥇 Best (highest, most upright) | 🥉 Smaller, front-half mainly | **🥈 Best for upward visibility / traffic lights** |
> | **Enyaq Coupé** | 🥇 Same platform, high position | 🥇 Full-length standard | **🥇 Potentially best of all** (different body) |
> | **EV6 GT-Line** | 🥉 Worst (raked, low) | 🥉 Moonroof, smallest | **🥉 Weakest on this priority** |
>
> **Key trade-off:** The Ioniq 5 has the best roof (and best for kids in the back enjoying the light) but the Enyaq likely has the best windscreen for looking upward. The EV6 loses on both. The Enyaq Coupé could be best of all worlds but is a different body style (lower rear roofline, less rear headroom).
>
> **Confidence note on windscreen ranking:** The Enyaq-best ranking is inferred from seating height and body profile (multiple reviews confirm Enyaq sits highest and has the most upright profile). No reviewer has specifically tested "can you see overhead traffic lights" across these cars. The EV6-worst ranking is better supported — its windscreen was specifically criticised for obscuring views at junctions.

## Features not available on ANY of these cars

- **Fold-out rear tables** — these were standard on the Picasso and are genuinely not a thing in modern EVs. Suggest: aftermarket tablet holders that clip to the headrests.
- **Hill start assist** — not needed in EVs. EVs hold automatically on hills via the motor (they don't roll back). Not a missing feature, just not necessary.

## Predictive ACC — key details

All three cars have nav-based predictive ACC that automatically slows for curves, roundabouts, speed limits, and junctions — without driver input. Same concept, different names:

- **Ioniq 5**: Navigation-based Smart Cruise Control (NSCC) — standard on ALL trims
- **EV6**: Navigation-based Smart Cruise Control with Curve (NSCC-C) — standard on ALL trims
- **Enyaq**: predictive ACC (pACC) — comes with Assisted Drive package. If an Enyaq has ACC, it has pACC (the ACC hardware is predictive by design). pACC is slightly broader than NSCC/NSCC-C — it also handles roundabouts and junctions, not just highway curves.

All three also have **traffic sign recognition** as standard on every trim. The camera reads speed limit signs and shows them in the instrument cluster. With predictive ACC active, the car also adjusts its speed to match the detected limit.
