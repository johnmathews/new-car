# Financial Comparison — Cost Analysis

_Last updated: March 2026_

## Three scenarios

| Scenario | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **A: Keep the Picasso** | Status quo. Continue driving, maintain, pay for petrol. |
| **B: Buy a large EV** | Sell Picasso, buy Ioniq 5 / EV6 / Enyaq. Main recommendation. |
| **C: Small EV + rental** | Buy a cheap small EV (Zoe/Leaf) for daily use, rent a larger car for holidays. |

**Conclusion up front:** Scenario B saves roughly **€10,500–14,400 over 5 years** vs keeping the Picasso (~€175–240/month). Over 3 years the saving is smaller but the EV is still cheaper in total once resale values are included.

---

## ⚠️ Road tax correction (updated March 2026)

The previous version of this document assumed €0 road tax for EVs. **This is no longer correct.**

The full BEV road tax (MRB) exemption ended on 1 January 2026. From 2026–2028, EVs pay **70% of the equivalent petrol rate** (i.e. 30% discount). From 2029 the discount falls to 25%, and from 2030 there is no discount.

For a mid-size EV (Ioniq 5, EV6, Enyaq, ~2,000–2,200 kg), this works out to approximately **€350–450/year** in road tax from 2026.

A comparable petrol family car pays a similar amount (EVs are heavier so taxed at a higher weight band, the discount roughly offsets this). The net road tax difference between Scenario A and B is therefore small — roughly **neutral** in the comparison. Road tax has been added to both ICE and EV costs in the figures below at ~€400/year.

**Notes:**

- Maintenance costs may be understated if major repairs are needed.

## Can an ICE car compete over 3 years?

This was a specific question. Comparing to buying a good quality used ICE family car (e.g. VW Touran, Seat Alhambra, ~€13,000) instead of an EV:

**Buy used ICE at €13,000:**

| Cost | Annual | 3-Year |
|------|--------|--------|
| Fuel | €3,000 | €9,000 |
| Insurance | €850 | €2,550 |
| Servicing | €700 | €2,100 |
| Road tax | €400 | €1,200 |
| Tyres | €300 | €900 |
| **Ongoing total** | **€5,250** | **€15,750** |

- Net outlay (after Picasso sale): €13,000 − €8,500 = **€4,500**
- Resale at year 3: ~€7,500–9,000
- **Total 3-year cost: €4,500 + €15,750 − €8,250 = ~€12,000**

**Compare to EV: ~€7,476**

The EV is still ~€4,500 cheaper over 3 years, even against a good-value used ICE. The reason: the EV's much better resale value (~€16,000 vs ~€8,250) more than offsets the higher upfront outlay.

### What would it take for an ICE to win over 3 years?

- Petrol would need to drop to ~€1.20–1.40/L sustainably (not realistic in NL)
- Or EV resale values would need to collapse significantly (possible but not current trend)
- Or electricity would need to rise above €0.40/kWh (possible, but night tariffs mitigate this)

**Conclusion: An ICE cannot realistically beat the EV over 3 years in the NL market, under any plausible scenario.** The EV's strong resale value is the key factor — it means the "payback period" on the upfront premium is effectively within the first 2 years when resale is included.

## Scenario C: Small EV + Holiday Rental

| Cost | Annual | 3-Year | 5-Year |
|------|--------|--------|--------|
| Electricity (18,000 km × 15kWh × €0.22) | €594 | €1,782 | €2,970 |
| Insurance (small EV) | €720 | €2,160 | €3,600 |
| Servicing | €250 | €750 | €1,250 |
| Holiday rental (2 × (€350 + €80 fuel)) | €860 | €2,580 | €4,300 |
| Road tax | €300 | €900 | €1,500 |
| **Ongoing total** | **€2,724** | **€8,172** | **€13,620** |

Capital: €13,000 − €8,500 = €4,500 net outlay
Resale: ~€7,000 at year 3

**Net 3-year cost: €4,500 + €8,172 − €7,000 = ~€5,672**

Looks cheaper than Scenario B on paper — but see the qualitative drawbacks below.

**Why Scenario C is less appealing:**
- Small car for 48 weeks of the year
- Rental adds friction and planning, especially during school holiday peaks
- Small EVs (Zoe, Leaf) have limited range and comfort
- Rental costs will rise; you can't lock them in

## Road tax trajectory (2026–2030)

| Year | EV discount | EV pays | Notes |
|------|------------|---------|-------|
| 2025 | 75% off | 25% of normal | Full exemption era ended |
| 2026–2028 | 30% off | 70% of normal | Current — ~€350–450/yr for our cars |
| 2029 | 25% off | 75% of normal | Slight increase |
| 2030+ | No discount | 100% of normal | EVs pay same as ICE |

From 2030, EVs will pay full road tax. On current rates this would be ~€500–600/year for a vehicle of this weight. This is factored into the long-term cost but doesn't change the 5-year conclusion materially.
