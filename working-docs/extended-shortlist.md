# Extended Shortlist — All 7 Cars Ranked

_Last updated: March 2026_

This document ranks all seven cars considered against the family's requirements. Two are outright ruled out (not available or way over budget). One is conditionally viable. The original three remain the active shortlist.

---

## The requirements (summary)

- Budget: ideally <€20k, hard ceiling ~€30k
- Family of 4 in Spaarndam
- ~20,000 km/year, mostly local + holiday trips (UK, Belgium)
- Must-haves: ACC, reversing/360° camera, parking assist, panoramic roof
- Nice-to-haves: electric tailgate, leather, wireless charging
- Values: space, comfort, quiet cabin, low running costs

---

## Rankings

### #1 — Hyundai Ioniq 5 Lounge/Ultimate (€24,000–29,000) ✅ ACTIVE

**Why it's #1:** Best balance of features, comfort, price, and reliability for this family. The key differentiator is that ACC and electric tailgate are standard on every trim — you can't accidentally buy an underpowered spec. The quietest cabin of the shortlist, 800V fast charging, and a 10-year battery warranty make it the lowest-risk choice.

The Lounge trim hits the sweet spot: adds panoramic roof and 360° camera without going to Ultimate pricing.

**Actively pursue: yes.**

---

### #2 — Kia EV6 GT-Line (€27,000–32,000) ✅ ACTIVE

**Why it's #2:** Marginally more features standard than the Ioniq 5, best audio system, and best rear legroom — but consistently €3,000–5,000 more for equivalent spec. Everything the Ioniq 5 does, the EV6 does equally well or slightly better, just at a higher price.

Worth pursuing if a GT-Line appears at a good price. If it's pushing €30,000+, the Ioniq 5 Lounge is better value.

**Actively pursue: yes, opportunistically.**

---

### #3 — Renault Scenic E-Tech Iconic (€22,000–27,000) ✅ ACTIVE

**Why it's #3:** The overlooked contender. Car of the Year 2024, longest real-world range of any car on this list (~620 km), sensible physical controls (no all-screen UX like Tesla), competitive pricing with the Ioniq 5 Lounge. Shares one weakness with the Enyaq — no electric tailgate — but brings better range and arguably a more family-focused interior.

Main drawback vs Ioniq 5: smaller boot (430 L vs ~490 L), slower DC charging (150 kW, ~30 min vs 18 min). Worth a test drive.

**Actively pursue: yes — add to test drive list.**

---

### #4 — Skoda Enyaq iV Edition/L&K (€18,000–27,000) ✅ ACTIVE (with reservation)

**Why it's #3:** The largest boot (585 L), lowest price, tri-zone climate, and best pre-conditioning flexibility. If the electric tailgate genuinely isn't a dealbreaker, the Enyaq offers excellent value and the most Picasso-like practicality.

The reservation: no electric tailgate on any trim. If that's important to your wife especially (hands full with kids/shopping), it should be dropped. If it's accepted as a trade-off for a bigger boot and lower price, it stays in the running.

**Actively pursue: yes, but confirm the tailgate question first.**

---

### #5 — Tesla Model Y (€28,000–35,000) ⚠️ CONDITIONAL

**Why it's #4:** Objectively one of the best EVs in this size class. Biggest boot of all seven cars (971 L), excellent real-world range, the most reliable fast-charging network in Europe (Supercharger), and strong used value. On pure capability it arguably deserves to be higher.

The reason it sits at #4 is the UX. The all-touchscreen interface (no instrument cluster, no physical stalks on 2024+ models), no CarPlay or Android Auto, and Tesla's direct-service model are genuine lifestyle changes. These aren't small things — they affect every single journey. Some families adapt and love it; others find it actively irritating after years.

**This car should only be pursued after a test drive.** If the interface is acceptable, it moves up to #2 or #3 on capability and value grounds.

**Actively pursue: only after a test drive to evaluate the UX.**

---

### #6 — Volkswagen ID.4 Style/Max (€21,000–28,000) ✅ SUPPLEMENTARY

**Why it's here:** Same MEB platform as the Enyaq, similar pricing and charging speed, but with one key advantage — **the Style and Max trims include an electric tailgate**, which the Enyaq never offers. Boot is 543 L (smaller than Enyaq's 585 L but larger than most others). Adds meaningful volume to the used car search with a large NL dealer network and plenty of Marktplaats listings.

Not a standout over the top four, but a sensible fallback and worth filtering for when browsing.

**Sweet spot trim:** Style (electric tailgate, pano roof, 360° cam in one trim)

**Actively pursue: supplementary — add to Marktplaats filters.**

---

### #7 — Nissan Ariya Evolve 87 kWh (€28,000–35,000) ⚠️ SUPPLEMENTARY / BUDGET CONCERN

**Why it's here:** Premium interior feel, full feature set on the Evolve trim, and wireless CarPlay standard. Adds more listings to browse. The concerns are price (87 kWh model is at/above budget), slowest DC charging on the list (130 kW, ~40 min), and very limited towing (750 kg). Worth a look if an 87 kWh example appears under €27,000.

**Actively pursue: only at ≤€27,000 for the 87 kWh version. Skip the 63 kWh.**

---

### #9 — Skoda Elroq (€33,000+) ❌ NOT VIABLE

**Why it's ruled out:** Newer, smaller, and more expensive than the Enyaq — which is already #3. It doesn't have an electric tailgate (same structural problem as Enyaq), has a smaller boot (470 L vs 585 L), and barely any used stock exists to bring prices below ~€33,000. There's no scenario where you'd choose the Elroq over the Enyaq, and it's firmly over budget.

**Ruled out: over budget, worse spec than Enyaq, no used market yet.**

---

### #10 — Kia EV9 (€59,000–79,000) ❌ NOT VIABLE

**Why it's ruled out:** An excellent car in the wrong price bracket. The three-row configuration is appealing if you regularly carry more than four people, but at two to three times the budget ceiling there is no path to viability. Used prices have not come down — and won't meaningfully for several years.

**Ruled out: price is approximately double the budget ceiling.**

---

### #11 — Volvo EX60 ❌ NOT AVAILABLE

**Why it's ruled out:** Doesn't exist yet. Revealed in January 2026, available to order in late spring 2026, first deliveries second half of 2026. No used market for years. On paper it looks very impressive (800V, up to 400 mile range, Volvo quality), but it's irrelevant for a purchase decision in 2026.

**Ruled out: can't be bought.**
