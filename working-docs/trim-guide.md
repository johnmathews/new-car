# Trim Level Guide

_Last updated: March 2026_

A quick reference for which trim level to look for when browsing used listings. All prices are approximate used market prices in the Netherlands (March 2026).

---

## Hyundai Ioniq 5

| Trim | Used price | Key features | Verdict |
|------|-----------|-------------|---------|
| SE Connect | €20,000–23,000 | ACC, electric tailgate, 8" display, basic spec | Too basic — no pano roof or 360° cam |
| Connect | €22,000–25,000 | Adds 12" nav display | Still no pano roof |
| **Lounge** ★ | **€24,000–28,000** | **Pano roof, 360° camera, heat pump, larger wheels** | **Sweet spot — gets all the key wishlist items** |
| Ultimate | €27,000–32,000 | Leather seats, HUD, self-parking assist, V2L | Nice if budget allows; leather and self-parking are the main adds |

**What to look for on listings:** Lounge or higher. SE Connect and Connect miss the panoramic roof and 360° camera.

**Note:** Heat pump is standard from Lounge trim. Always verify on the specific listing.

**Minimum acceptable:** Lounge

## Kia EV6

| Trim | Used price | Key features | Verdict |
|------|-----------|-------------|---------|
| Air | €20,000–23,000 | ACC, electric tailgate, basic spec | Too basic — no pano, no camera |
| Wind | €23,000–26,000 | Adds 360° camera, larger battery standard | Closer but still missing pano roof |
| **GT-Line** ★ | **€27,000–32,000** | **Pano roof, parking assist, Meridian audio, leather standard** | **Sweet spot — but stretches budget** |
| GT-Line S | €32,000–38,000 | Adds cooled seats, HUD, 21" wheels | Over budget |

**What to look for on listings:** GT-Line. Wind is acceptable but misses the panoramic roof. GT-Line includes leather as standard (not an option), which is a bonus.

**Note:** The EV6 GT-Line consistently costs €3,000–5,000 more than an equivalent Ioniq 5 Lounge. Worth keeping in mind.

**Minimum acceptable:** Wind (if GT-Line is over budget, noting no pano roof)

## Tips when browsing listings

1. **Always check the trim level explicitly** — sellers sometimes don't list it correctly, or list a car as "fully loaded" when it's a mid-spec.
2. **Cross-reference with the VIN or option codes** — Hyundai and Kia dealers can look up the original spec from the VIN. Useful for second-hand verification.
3. **Look for factory sticker / window sticker** — some sellers have the original order sheet which lists every option.
4. **Marktplaats filter tips:** Filter by year (2021+), fuel type (elektrisch), and set a max price. Then manually check trim level from the listing description.
5. **AutoScout24** is also worth checking alongside Marktplaats — sometimes different inventory.
