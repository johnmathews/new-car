# Pre-Purchase Checklist — Used EV

_Last updated: April 2026_
_Applies to: Hyundai Ioniq 5, Kia EV6, Skoda Enyaq (and most used EVs generally)_

Use this when you're viewing a specific car. Print it out or have it on your phone.

---

## Before you go

- [ ] **RDW.nl check** — enter the licence plate at rdw.nl. Verify: registration matches the car, no outstanding recalls, APK (MOT) status, import history, number of previous owners.
- [ ] **Check trim level** — confirm the specific listing matches the trim you expect (Lounge vs Connect, GT-Line vs Wind, etc.). Ask seller to confirm exact trim in writing before visiting.
- [ ] **Establish the generation** — all three cars have had mid-cycle facelifts that change things significantly. Before visiting, ask the seller or check the registration date (see below). Pre-facelift and facelift cars are priced differently and have different features.
- [ ] **Service history** — ask if there's a full stamped service book or digital service records. For EVs, look for software update history, any battery-related work, charging port servicing.

---

## Generation check — do this before or on arrival

All three shortlisted cars have facelifts. Ask or verify before driving.

### Skoda Enyaq
- **Pre-facelift (2021–2024):** Registered 2024 or earlier. Sluggish software (version up to 4.0), no electric tailgate, blind spot monitoring is an option not standard. Cheaper.
- **Facelift (2025+):** Registered from ~April 2025. Software 6.0 on new hardware (Smart Dials), electric tailgate standard on ALL trims, blind spot standard. Pricier.
- **Quick check:** Is there an electric tailgate? Does the infotainment have physical Smart Dials knobs? If yes to both → facelift.

### Hyundai Ioniq 5
- **Pre-facelift (2021–2024):** 77.4 kWh battery, older infotainment, no rear wiper.
- **Facelift (2025 MY):** 84 kWh battery, new ccNC infotainment (wireless CarPlay/AA built in), rear wiper present, new-style interior buttons and centre console.
- **Quick check:** Is there a rear wiper? Ask "is this the 77 or 84 kWh battery?" — that's the fastest tell. Check if wireless CarPlay/AA is native (not via a dongle).

### Kia EV6
- **Pre-facelift (2021–2024):** 77.4 kWh battery, older infotainment, charging port on the **right** rear fender.
- **Facelift (2025 MY):** 84 kWh battery, new ccNC infotainment, charging port moved to **left** rear fender, fingerprint start, 10 airbags (up from 7), Star Map headlights.
- **Quick check:** Walk to the rear and look at which side the charging port is on. Right = pre-facelift. Left = facelift. It's that simple.

## At the viewing

### Battery & charging

- [ ] **Battery health report (SoH)** — ask for a printout. Any competent EV dealer or independent EV specialist can produce this. Target: >85% state of health for cars under 100,000 km. Below 80% is a red flag.
- [ ] **Battery warning history** — ask if any battery-related warning lights have appeared. Ask them to show you the car's fault history via OBD diagnostic if possible.
- [ ] **Battery coolant condition** — ask when it was last checked/changed (especially relevant on older examples).
- [ ] **Test DC fast charging during viewing** — actually plug the car into a fast charger if one is nearby and watch the charge rate. It should ramp up quickly and hold near its rated max. Throttling early = battery issue.
- [ ] **Charging port condition** — visually inspect the CCS (DC) and Type 2 (AC) ports. No bent pins, no corrosion. Test both with your own cable if possible.
- [ ] **Heat pump** — confirm it's fitted. This is critical for Dutch winters and significantly affects range in cold weather. It's not standard on all trims. Ask to see it demonstrated (turn on climate with external temp low).

### Ioniq 5 / EV6 specific

- [ ] **12V auxiliary battery** — ask if it's been replaced. This is a known weak point on both cars. A failed 12V can strand you even with a full main battery. Original 12V on a 3–4 year old car = it may be due for replacement.
- [ ] **ICCU module (Ioniq 5)** — ask about Integrated Charging Control Unit history. Early Ioniq 5 units had failures. Check if any ICCU-related service was done.
- [ ] **Software version** — check if the car is on the latest firmware. Bluelink / Kia Connect should show this in the app or vehicle settings.

### Body & mechanical

- [ ] **Underside battery check** — look underneath for any damage to the battery pack. Ask if the car has been in any accidents (cross-reference with RDW and ask for insurance history if possible).
- [ ] **Tyre condition** — EVs are heavier and wear tyres 20–30% faster than ICE cars. Check tread depth on all four. Uneven wear may indicate alignment or suspension issues.
- [ ] **Uneven tyre wear pattern** — if inner or outer edge is worn faster, this suggests an alignment or suspension problem. Worth factoring into negotiation.
- [ ] **Accident history** — check for panel colour mismatches, overspray in door jambs, uneven panel gaps. Ask directly about any previous repairs.

### Features & app

- [ ] **Test the app** (MySkoda / Bluelink / Kia Connect) — ask the seller to demonstrate remote pre-conditioning and charging status via the app. Confirm the subscription is active and transferable.
- [ ] **Verify must-have features work** — test predictive ACC (set it on a road — it should adjust speed for curves, speed limits, and junctions), panoramic roof (open and close it if it opens — **this is now a big priority**), reversing camera, park assist, electric tailgate if present. Don't assume they work.
- [ ] **Test park assist** — if the car has it, ask the seller to demonstrate parallel parking assist. The car should measure a space and steer itself in; you handle the pedals. Feature names: Ioniq 5/EV6 = Smart Parking Assist (SPA), Enyaq = Park Assist (part of Parking Plus package).
- [ ] **Panoramic roof type** — before the viewing, confirm whether the roof opens. Ioniq 5 = fixed (light only, no fresh air). EV6 = tilts. Enyaq SUV = opens fully (but only if the option was specced). See feature-comparison.md for full detail.
- [ ] **App subscription status** — some connected features (Bluelink, Kia Connect) require an active subscription. Ask if it's paid up and how long remains.

### Enyaq-specific checks

- [ ] **Assisted Drive package** — does the car have ACC? If so, it has predictive ACC (pACC) which automatically adjusts for curves, roundabouts, speed limits, and junctions using nav data. pACC also includes Traffic Jam Assist, Blind Spot Detection, and Crew Protect Assist.
- [ ] **Side Assist package** — does the car have blind spot monitoring with indicators in the mirrors? This comes from the Side Assist package, which also includes rear cross-traffic alert and exit warning. NOT standard on pre-2025 Enyaq.
- [ ] **Parking Plus package** — does the car have Park Assist (auto steering into spaces) and a 360° Area View Camera? These come from the Parking Plus package, which also includes Trained Parking. NOT standard on any pre-2025 Enyaq trim. Check for it specifically.
- [ ] **Three separate packages** — Assisted Drive, Side Assist, and Parking Plus are three separate option packages. A car can have one without the others. Business Edition models often have all three, but ALWAYS check.

## Questions to ask the seller

**Generation / model year:**
1. "What model year is this — is it the pre-facelift or facelifted version?"
2. *(Ioniq 5 / EV6)* "Is this the 77 kWh or 84 kWh battery?"
3. *(Enyaq)* "What software version is it running — is it version 6.0?"
4. "Can you confirm the exact trim level in writing?"

**Battery & technical:**

5. "Can I see the battery health report?"
6. "Has the 12V auxiliary battery ever been replaced?"
7. "Has the car had any battery-related warning lights or service?"
8. "Is the heat pump fitted on this car?"
9. "Can I test the DC fast charging right now?"

**Features (Enyaq-specific):**

10. "Does this car have the Assisted Drive package?" (gives predictive ACC + blind spot)
11. "Does it have the Side Assist package?" (blind spot indicators in mirrors + rear cross-traffic alert)
12. "Does it have the Parking Plus package?" (Park Assist auto-steering + 360° camera + Trained Parking)

**History & paperwork:**

13. "What's the reason for selling?"
14. "Is there a full service history?"
15. "Has the car been in any accidents?"
16. "Is the connected car app subscription active?"

## Red flags — walk away if you see these

- SoH below 80%
- Seller refuses to show battery health report or service history
- Evidence of battery warning lights with no documented repair
- Significant underside damage
- Charging port pins bent or corroded
- DC fast charging rate severely throttled during test
- Accident history with no documentation

---

## Good signs

- Full digital service history with software updates logged
- Battery SoH above 90%
- 12V battery recently replaced (proactive maintenance = careful owner)
- Seller has original purchase invoice and can walk you through the history
- One or two careful previous owners
- *(Enyaq)* Car has all three option packages (Assisted Drive + Side Assist + Parking Plus) — "Business Edition" spec often includes all three

---

## After purchase

- Transfer registration at RDW immediately
- Notify your insurer the same day
- Set up the connected car app with your own account
- Book an independent EV health check within the first month (~€150 at a good EV specialist)
