# Kia EV9 — Assessment

_Last updated: March 2026_
_Verdict: **Not viable — budget is the hard stop**_

## The appeal

The EV9 is genuinely exciting on paper. It's Kia's flagship SUV with a proper three-row, seven-seat configuration — the only car on this list that could carry the whole extended family or a group of friends. It shares the 800V platform with the EV6, has excellent range, and is very well equipped.

If budget weren't a constraint, it would absolutely be on the shortlist.

## Why it doesn't work

**Price.** Used examples in the Netherlands are currently listed at **€59,000–79,000**. That's roughly double the upper end of the budget (€30,000). There's no realistic path to finding one within budget — these cars are too new and too expensive for the used market to have brought prices down meaningfully yet.

## Key specs (for reference)

| | |
|---|---|
| Battery | 99.8 kWh |
| Range (WLTP) | ~500 km |
| Charging | 800V, up to 233 kW DC |
| Seats | 6 or 7 (three rows) |
| Boot | 333 L (7-seat) / 828 L (6-seat, third row folded) |
| Used price (NL) | €59,000–79,000 |

## Verdict

Ruled out on budget. Unless there's a dramatic rethink of what you're willing to spend, this isn't the car.

The three-row aspect is interesting if you regularly need to carry more than four people — but for a family of two adults and two kids, a five-seat car is fine and doesn't justify doubling the budget.
