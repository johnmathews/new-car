## ⚠️ Important: Pre-facelift vs Facelift (2025)

The Enyaq had a significant mid-cycle facelift revealed on **8 January 2025**, with Dutch orders opening 9 January 2025 and NL deliveries starting approximately April 2025. The two generations differ meaningfully — see `model-year-guide.md` for the full comparison across all three cars.

| | Pre-facelift (2021–2024) | Facelift (2025+) |
|---|---|---|
| **Software** | Versions up to 4.0 — widely criticised as sluggish; hardware cannot be upgraded | Software 6.0 on new hardware — significantly faster. Smart Dials (3 physical knobs with colour displays) |
| **Electric tailgate** | ❌ Not available on any trim | ✅ Standard on ALL trims |
| **Blind spot monitoring** | ⚠️ Option only (Side Assist package) | ✅ Standard on all trims |
| **Range (WLTP)** | ~534 km | ~589 km (better aero, same battery) |
| **Trim lineup** | Base, Edition, Sportline, L&K | SE L, Edition, Sportline (L&K dropped) |
| **New price (NL)** | — | From ~€42,990 |
| **How to identify** | Registered 2024 or earlier | Registered from ~April 2025 |

**Key implication for used buying:** A 2025-registered Enyaq in the Netherlands is likely facelift. Any 2024 or earlier is pre-facelift. Facelift models will be newer and significantly more expensive on the used market (likely €35,000+ in spring 2026, likely above budget). Pre-facelift models offer better value but the software is a real-world frustration for many owners.

## Why it's #3

The Enyaq is great value and has the biggest boot — the most Picasso-like in terms of practicality. The 400V platform means slower DC charging than the Ioniq 5 or EV6 (36 min for 10–80% vs ~18 min) — not a dealbreaker but relevant for the longer holiday trips. Too many must-haves are locked behind optional packages on Gen 1; Gen 2 addresses some of this.

## ⚠️ Enyaq option packages — critical for used car shopping

On a pre-2025 Enyaq, three separate option packages cover most of the must-have ADAS features. They are independent — a car can have one without the others. Always check which packages are fitted.

| Package | What it includes | Standard? |
|---------|-----------------|-----------|
| **Assisted Drive** | pACC + Traffic Jam Assist + Blind Spot Detection + Crew Protect Assist | ❌ Option |
| **Side Assist** | Blind spot indicators in mirrors + rear cross-traffic alert + exit warning | ❌ Option |
| **Parking Plus** | Park Assist (auto steering) + 360° Area View Camera + Trained Parking | ❌ Option — NOT on any pre-2025 trim |

**Business Edition** models often have all three packages, but always verify. The 2025 facelift made Side Assist and pACC standard, but Park Assist is still optional.

## Unique advantages over Ioniq 5 and EV6

These are things the Enyaq does that the others don't:

1. **Biggest boot:** 585 L vs ~490 L — most practical for family trips, closest to the Picasso
2. **Tri-zone climate control:** Independent rear climate zone. Kids can set their own temperature.
3. **Rear window blinds:** Standard on Edition and above. Others need aftermarket magnetic shades.
4. **Scheduled pre-heating without charging:** Can warm the car up at a set time even if it finished charging hours ago. Ioniq 5 and EV6 require active charging for scheduled heat.
5. **Cornering lights:** Fog lights double as cornering lights — better illumination in bends.
6. **Canton audio:** 12–14 speakers with subwoofer, comparable to Meridian in EV6.

## Panoramic glass options — important detail ⭐

Since light and a sense of space is a top priority, the Enyaq's panoramic situation is more nuanced than it first appears.

**Regular Enyaq SUV (the one John test drove):**
- Panoramic sunroof is an **optional extra** on Edition trim and above — not standard, must be specced
- Reviewer notes it **doesn't extend far over the rear seats** — a child in a rear-facing rear seat may not even see through it
- Covers mainly the front of the cabin; smaller than it sounds
- Ranks #3 of the four options below (behind Ioniq 5, ahead of EV6)
- Ask any seller explicitly: "Does this car have the panoramic sunroof option?"

**Enyaq Coupé (different body style — not the SUV):**
- Full-length fixed glass roof, **standard on all trims** — the largest panoramic roof in Skoda's history
- Runs the entire length of the roof; described as running "from B-pillar to rear"
- No sunblind — glass is heat-treated and tinted instead
- This is a different, lower, more sloping car than the SUV; trade-offs include less rear headroom

**Ranked by panoramic roof glass (best to worst):**
1. 🥇 **Enyaq Coupé** — full-length standard, largest of all. Different body style.
2. 🥈 **Ioniq 5 (Lounge+)** — large fixed vision roof, no cross-bars, best of the three SUV/crossovers
3. 🥉 **Enyaq regular SUV (with option)** — smaller than expected, covers front mainly
4. **EV6 (GT-Line)** — moonroof, smallest of the four, also loses 56mm headroom

**But the Enyaq wins on windscreen / upward visibility:**
The Enyaq (both SUV and Coupé) has the highest, most upright driving position of the three shortlisted cars. Multiple reviews confirm you sit noticeably higher than in an Ioniq 5 or EV6. This means better ability to see overhead traffic lights, signs, and sky through the windscreen — the closest you'll get to the Picasso's Zenith windscreen experience among these three. None of the three have a true panoramic windscreen like the Picasso's Zenith, but the Enyaq's higher, more upright position compensates the most.

## Test drive impressions (1 April 2026)

John's first time behind the wheel of an EV. Key takeaways:

- **Driving experience:** EV driving is noticeably easier than ICE. The simplicity of the drivetrain and instant response stood out.
- **ACC:** Much better than in the Picasso. Reinforces ACC as the #1 must-have feature.
- **Cabin noise:** Noticeably quieter than the Picasso — confirmed the spec-sheet claims in practice.
- **Music / audio:** Sounds better than in the Picasso — the quieter cabin makes a big difference to audio quality, even before considering the Canton system vs the Picasso's factory speakers.
- **Panoramic windscreen / sunroof:** This is a BIG priority. After experiencing it (or seeing the option), John rates this very highly — moved up in feature priority list.

Overall: positive experience. The EV format itself impressed — this bodes well for all three shortlisted cars, not just the Enyaq.

## Comfort & cabin

- **Noise level:** ~67–68 dB at motorway speed — comparable to Ioniq 5, slightly better than EV6. **Confirmed in test drive: noticeably quieter than the Picasso.**
- **Space:** Largest boot (585 L). Rear legroom is good but not quite EV6 level.
- **Interior feel:** More traditional/conservative than Ioniq 5. VW Group quality feel.

---

## Charging specifics

- **AC (home):** Up to 11 kW. Full charge overnight (~7 hours from 20%)
- **DC (fast):** Up to 135 kW (400V platform). 10–80% in ~36 minutes
- This is the main charging disadvantage vs Ioniq 5/EV6. For a quick top-up on a holiday route, you're stopping for ~36 min vs ~18 min.
- Compatible with Type 2 (AC) and CCS (DC)

---

## Electric tailgate — pre-facelift vs facelift

**Pre-facelift (2021–2024):** No electric tailgate on any trim at all.
**Facelift (2025+):** Electric tailgate with gesture (foot kick) activation is **standard on ALL trims**.

Since electric tailgate is a nice-to-have for John rather than a must, the pre-facelift not having it is acceptable. But it's worth knowing that the facelift solves this completely.
