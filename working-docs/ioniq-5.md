## ⚠️ Important: Pre-facelift vs Facelift (2025 MY)

The Ioniq 5 received a significant mid-cycle facelift for the **2025 model year**, launched globally July 2024 and arriving in Europe late 2024/early 2025. The two versions are meaningfully different — see `model-year-guide.md` for full details.

| | Pre-facelift (MY 2021–2024) | Facelift (MY 2025+) |
|---|---|---|
| **Battery** | 77.4 kWh gross / 72.6 kWh usable | **84 kWh gross** (~8% more range) |
| **Range (WLTP RWD)** | ~480 km | ~580 km |
| **Infotainment** | Older Hyundai system | New ccNC — wireless CarPlay/AA OTA updates |
| **Interior** | Original design | New climate controls, console, steering wheel |
| **Rear wiper** | ❌ Not present | ✅ Standard |
| **Wireless CarPlay** | Some trims | Standard (via ccNC) |
| **Remote seat folding** | ❌ | ✅ Via app |
| **Built-in dashcam** | ❌ | ✅ Standard |
| **How to identify** | Registered 2024 or earlier (mostly) | Registered from late 2024; 84 kWh battery is the clearest tell |

**Used car implication:** Most 2022–2024 Ioniq 5s on Marktplaats are pre-facelift. Some late-2024-registered cars may be facelift spec. A 2025-registered car is almost certainly facelift. When viewing, ask whether the battery is 77 kWh or 84 kWh — that's the quickest way to tell.

## Why this is the #1 pick

Every single trim level includes predictive ACC (NSCC), blind spot detection, traffic sign recognition, and electric tailgate as standard. When buying used, you don't have to check whether the seller spec'd those options — they're always there. That's a significant advantage when browsing listings.

The 800V platform is the other standout. At a fast charger (e.g. Fastned, Ionity), it charges at up to 233 kW, meaning a genuine 18-minute stop takes you from 10% to 80%. For the UK and Belgium trips, this matters.

## Panoramic vision roof — a genuine strength ⭐

The Ioniq 5's "vision roof" is **the best panoramic glass option among the three shortlisted cars** for the goal of maximum light and sense of space — including for rear-seat passengers (important: John wants the kids to enjoy being in the car). Key facts:

- One large glass panel with no cross-bar supports — unusually open and airy
- Reviewers consistently praise it: "incredibly expansive", "one of my favourite features", "makes the space feel very open"
- Covers front and centre of the roof — noticeable for both front and rear passengers
- Fixed (does not open), but since opening is not a priority, this is irrelevant
- Has a motorised sunblind so you can block sun when needed
- Does NOT reduce headroom (unlike the EV6 moonroof)
- Available from Lounge trim — a key reason Lounge is the sweet spot

**Compared to competitors:** Directly reviewed against the EV6 by owners — Ioniq 5 is "much brighter inside with the big pano roof" while "EV6 has smaller windows, darker interior, and a smaller moonroof". The Enyaq SUV's optional sunroof is also smaller (covers mainly the front, reviewers note it doesn't extend far to the rear).

**Windscreen / upward visibility note:** None of the three have a panoramic windscreen like the Picasso's Zenith. The Ioniq 5's windscreen angle is moderate — you sit higher than in the EV6 but lower than in the Enyaq. For seeing overhead traffic lights, the Enyaq's higher, more upright position is better. But the Ioniq 5's larger panoramic roof panel combined with reasonable windscreen visibility gives it the **best overall cabin brightness** of the three.

## Comfort & cabin

- **Noise level:** ~67 dB at motorway speed — quietest of the three main candidates
- **Space:** Good rear legroom, flat floor (skateboard platform). Not quite as roomy in the rear as EV6.
- **Frunk:** Small but useful for cables
- **Seats:** Comfortable on long trips. Heating standard, no cooling (cooling is over budget on other models anyway)

---

## Audio

Bose 8-speaker system. Sounds good but no subwoofer — weakest of the three premium audio options. Not a dealbreaker, but worth noting if audio quality matters.

---

## Climate & pre-conditioning

- Dual-zone climate control (front + rear)
- Can pre-heat cabin via the Bluelink app
- **Important:** Scheduled heating only works while actively charging. If the car finishes charging at 2am and you want it warm at 8am, you need to set the departure time rather than a timer.
- Heat pump standard on Lounge and above — significantly improves winter efficiency

---

## Known reliability issues — things to check

1. **12V auxiliary battery** — a known weak point. Ask if it's been replaced. Failure can strand the car even with a full main battery.
2. **ICCU module** (Integrated Charging Control Unit) — some early units had failures. Check for any related recalls or service history.
3. **Software updates** — Hyundai pushes OTA updates. Confirm the car is on latest firmware.
4. Battery health: expect >85% SoH on cars under 100,000 km.

---

## Charging specifics

- **AC (home):** Up to 11 kW on-board charger. Full charge overnight from ~20% takes ~6–7 hours.
- **DC (fast):** Up to 233 kW. 10–80% in ~18 minutes at a compatible charger (Fastned, Ionity).
- Compatible with all European standards: Type 2 (AC), CCS (DC)
- **V2L (Vehicle to Load):** Available on Ultimate — can power devices/appliances from the car
