# Renault Scenic E-Tech — Assessment

_Last updated: March 2026_
_Verdict: **Worth a look — the overlooked family car on this list**_

## What it is

The Scenic E-Tech is Renault's all-electric revival of the Scenic nameplate — this time as a proper family SUV rather than the old MPV. It won European Car of the Year 2024, beating some strong competition. It's a genuine family-focused design with a large battery, impressive range, and a thoughtful interior.

## Why it's interesting

**Range.** At ~620 km WLTP with the 87 kWh battery, it has the longest real-world range of any car on this list — including the Ioniq 5 and EV6. For the UK and Belgium trips, range anxiety is essentially zero.

**Price.** Used examples from 2023 should be entering the €22,000–27,000 range — comfortably within budget, and competitive with the Ioniq 5 Lounge.

**Car of the Year 2024.** It won the award against strong competition. The interior design is genuinely distinctive and family-friendly, with a clean dashboard layout and good ergonomics.

**Sensible design choices.** Physical shortcut buttons on the dashboard (Renault calls them OpenR Link shortcuts) — unlike Tesla, you don't have to dig through touchscreen menus for frequently used functions.

## Concerns

**400V platform.** DC charging maxes at 150 kW, putting 10–80% at around 30 minutes. That's slower than the Ioniq 5/EV6 (18 min) but faster than the Enyaq (36 min). Acceptable, not excellent.

**Boot.** At 430 L it's the smallest boot of any car seriously considered here — smaller than the Ioniq 5 (~490 L), EV6 (~490 L), Enyaq (585 L), or Model Y (971 L). For a family with kids' gear, this is worth noting.

**Towing.** Only 900 kg — the lowest of any car on this list. If you ever tow (bikes, a trailer, a small boat), this is limiting.

**Newer to market.** The Scenic E-Tech launched late 2023. The used market in NL is growing but not deep yet. Selection is limited compared to the Ioniq 5 or EV6.

**No electric tailgate.** Like the Enyaq, the Scenic doesn't offer a powered tailgate — worth noting given this is on the wishlist.

## Verdict

The Scenic E-Tech is a genuine contender that wasn't on the original shortlist and probably should have been. It competes mainly with the Enyaq — similar price, no electric tailgate, but better range and arguably better interior design. Its main weakness vs the Ioniq 5 is the slower charging and smaller boot.

Worth adding to a test drive list, especially if range is important to you or if you find the Ioniq 5 pricing is stretching.

**Recommended trim:** Iconic (87 kWh, panoramic roof, better spec)
